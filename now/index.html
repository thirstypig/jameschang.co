questions i’m pondering sports, mostly

⚾ baseball

  1. When does the 2027 MLB season actually start — and will the owners and the MLBPA reach a deal before it? The current CBA expires after the 2026 season, and the salary-cap talk has lockout written all over it. why it matters: a work stoppage would cost us Opening Day — and a season I build my year around.
  2. After every MLB game, both teams should come out to the middle of the field, or near home plate, and shake hands. Other sports do it; baseball should too. why it matters: a small ritual that models the sportsmanship the game keeps saying it wants.
  3. What if MLB went from 3 strikes and 4 balls to 2 strikes and 3 balls? Games would move faster and pitchers would wear down slower — one change, two problems solved. why it matters: pace of play and arm health are the league’s two biggest headaches, and this hits both.
  4. As teams spend more and more, going to a game keeps getting more prohibitive. Everyone says the Dodgers are wealthy with a bigger budget than most — but they’re not dumb. They’ll pass that cost to the fans; they won’t spend on players just to lose money. I’ve watched parking, tickets, and concessions all climb, and honestly I won’t be going to as many games as I used to. why it matters: if the ballpark prices out everyday fans, the sport loses the people who make it feel alive.
  5. When A-Rod joined the Yankees he’d already won a couple of Gold Gloves at shortstop — so why wasn’t Jeter the one asked to move to third base? It mirrors the Bregman signing in Boston with Devers at third: the better fit deferring to the incumbent. why it matters: it’s the eternal tug-of-war between résumé, ego, and what actually makes the team better.

🏀 the lakers

  1. Does LeBron James retire? I think he should — or come back next year at the league minimum, if winning is really the point. why it matters: how a top-five-ever player chooses to exit says everything about legacy versus ego.
  2. Can Luka Dončić take the Lakers to the promised land? Pairing him with the franchise is a swing for the fences. why it matters: it’s the whole post-LeBron bet — does the next era start here, or stall?
  3. Will the Lakers re-sign Austin Reaves — and at what price? Homegrown, overdelivering, and about to get expensive. why it matters: the classic cap-versus-loyalty call that decides whether you keep the players who built the culture.

🏈 rams & browns

  1. Will Myles Garrett still be a dominating force now that he’s on the Rams? New scheme, new division, same freak athlete. why it matters: a star changing teams is the real test of whether it was the player or the situation.
  2. Would Aaron Donald really make a comeback to the Rams now that Garrett is there? The two of them on one line is a fantasy worth pondering. why it matters: retirements aren’t always final, and the right teammate can change the math.
  3. Did the Browns make the right call — and did they get enough? I think they did: sell high on Garrett, bank the picks, and land a younger edge rusher in Jared Verse. why it matters: it’s the question every rebuild faces — when to cash in a star for the future.
