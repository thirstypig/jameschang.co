Aleph · Marketing · Last Updated April 15, 2026

Four steps from import to compliant.

No compliance background required. Aleph walks you through everything — from SKU catalog through generated certificate, warning label, or disclosure report.

The four steps

01 Foundation

Add your products.

Create your SKU catalog. Enter product names, descriptions, manufacturers, and country of origin. Import from CSV if you have a spreadsheet. Every SKU is the anchor for the compliance work that follows.

02 Assessment

Complete the compliance questionnaire.

For each product, answer guided questions specific to the regulation you need — FSVP, CPSIA, Prop 65, or PFAS. Pre-populated options and plain-language explanations keep you on track. No legal jargon.

03 Evidence

Upload supporting documents.

Attach lab test reports, certificates of analysis, supplier agreements, and other supporting files. Everything is linked directly to the product and the regulation it belongs to — so when an audit hits, the documentation chain is already assembled.

04 Output

Generate certificates, labels, and reports.

Click a button to generate the output you need — a CPC PDF, a Prop 65 warning label, a PFAS disclosure report, or an FSVP audit summary. Ready to file, print, or share.

What each module covers

One SKU catalog, four regulatory surfaces. Each module shares the same product + document spine but generates regulation-specific outputs.
